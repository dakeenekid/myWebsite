How yoyoing has changed my life...

Yes, you read that correctly. Yoyoing, the hobby of using and competing with yoyos, has irriversably changed my outlook on life.

When I was 10, I saw a yoyoist perform at my local library, and instantly knew that a passion of mine was to become as good as he was. He could walk the dog, he could walk the cat, and shoot the moon. I bought my first yoyo, the Yomega Maverick, and from then on I taught myself using determination and hours of YouTube and YoyoExpert.

I quickly learned that yoyoing was so much more than just a fun hobby. It was a global competition to become the best. So, for the next six years, I have developed my skills and yoyo mastery into what it is today: nothing short of amazing, and I quickly became intersted in the business aspect of the sport.

I created my own company, known as Tropic Spins, and partnered wih kids around the country to help it thrive and grow. We sold more than 250 yoyos nationally and became good friends. We learned how to run a business, how to market, and how to make instagram a great advertiser for products. We needed to make a website, and I was very intersted in technology, so I learned basic HTML and CSS to do the job, before giving up and using weebly instead. However, this sparked my interest in coding, and once I signed up for a codecademy course the rest was history. Simply put, the yoyo helped me achieve my accomplishments in technology, and I owe it to the hobby that I am able to develop my own programs.
